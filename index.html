Intro

Greetings, my name is Jason Zhang. My journey began in childhood, where I was fortunate to grow up surrounded by electronics. My fascination with the digital world started on my father's phone, amazed by how such a small device could hold so much content and connect with people across the globe.

Throughout middle school I was deeply invested in problem-solving games like chess, Rubik's cubes, and various puzzles. Come high school I naturally fell in love with coding, and my interest was instantly through the roof. Coding combined my passion for problem-solving with the digital world, perfectly blending my early love for electronics and communication. Since then, everything about software engineering has resonated with me, making it a natural extension of my lifelong interests.

Work

Developed a full-stack Amazon clone, creatively named "Amazeon," using Ruby on Rails and AWS S3 for the backend and, React/ Redux and HTML / CSS for the frontend.

Live Github

Live Github

As part of a team of four software engineers, I collaborated to create "Bonjour World," a language exchange platform designed to host language exchange events. We built this project using the MERN stack, incorporating MongoDB, Express.js, Ruby on Rails, and Node.js as its core technologies. Following the initial development, we integrated the Places API and Google Maps API to enhance the platform's functionality.

About

In addition to my professional development, I've been learning to cook and expanding my palate. I also enjoy going to the gym, focusing mainly on my arms and back. Watching the sunset is one of my favorite ways to unwind, as the setting is incredibly comfortable and relaxing. I used to be an avid gamer, playing a variety of games from FPS titles like Valorant to 5v5 matches in League of Legends and battle royales like Apex Legends. To top it all off, I play chess, as I love strategizing and solving complex problems.

I love the feeling of understanding and grasping a concept fully. The satisfaction of fixing a bug or successfully implementing a function keeps my passion for software engineering alive. In addition I am very curious about AI and how LLM's function and learn!
